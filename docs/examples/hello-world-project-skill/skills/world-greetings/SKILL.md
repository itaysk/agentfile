---
name: world-greetings
description: How to greet in different cultures and languages
---

| Culture or language | Greeting | How to use it |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Hawaii | `Aloha` | Use for hello, goodbye, affection, and goodwill. Say it warmly rather than treating it as only a tourist phrase. |
| Japan | `Konnichiwa` | Use as a polite daytime hello. A small bow can accompany the greeting in formal or respectful settings. |
| India | `Namaste` | Use as a respectful hello. It may be paired with palms together at the chest, especially in more traditional or formal contexts. |
| Arabic-speaking cultures | `As-salamu alaykum` | Use as a peaceful greeting meaning "peace be upon you." The common reply is `Wa alaykum as-salam`. |
| Spain and Latin America | `Hola` | Use as a general hello. In casual settings, people may add a friendly phrase like `Como estas?` depending on region and familiarity. |
| France | `Bonjour` | Use during the day in shops, offices, and first meetings. Prefer `Bonsoir` in the evening. |
